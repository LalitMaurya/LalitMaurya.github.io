Africa’s heat waves are getting worse due to human activities: Study

Global Plastic Profiles 2025: Article 20 establishes the COP as the central governing body of the Treaty
While there is broad agreement on the COP’s necessity, the negotiations reflect differing views on how expansive its authority should be
 
   
 
Global Plastic Profiles 2025: Article 19 addresses health impacts of plastic pollution
Countries are divided between embedding health language throughout the treaty or creating a standalone article
 
   
 
Global Plastic Profiles 2025: Article 18 focuses on fostering public understanding, stakeholder engagement and scientific progress for effective implementation of Treaty
It recognises that tackling plastic pollution extends beyond policy and infrastructure
 
   
 
Global Plastic Profiles 2025: Article 17, on information exchange, enjoys general support in principle
The challenge will be to ensure that information exchange is both functional and inclusive, enabling Parties to act on shared knowledge without undermining sovereignty or operational feasibility
 

 
   
   
 
Global Plastic Profiles 2025: Article 16 outlines assessing how well the Treaty achieves its objectives over time
Parties have differing views; but most agree on the importance of systematic evaluation for continuous improvement
 
   
 
Global Plastic Profiles 2025: Article 15 outlines how Parties will report their efforts to implement the Treaty
While many countries supported the general intent of the article, they diverged on how binding and detailed the obligations should be
 
   
 
Global Plastic Profile 2025: Article 14, on national plans, is contentious among countries
While the concept of national planning received broad acknowledgment, the level of obligation and prescriptiveness around these plans remains a point of contention among states
 
   
 
Global Plastic Profiles 2025: Article 13, on implementation and compliance, has sparked debate
The proposed mechanism includes the formation of a committee tasked with supporting implementation and promoting adherence to treaty obligations
 
   
 
Global Plastic Profiles 2025: Article 11, on the means of implementation, is a complex and unresolved area of the Treaty text
The primary divergence lies in the question of the financial mechanism
 
   
 
Global Plastic Profiles 2025: Article 10 aims at just transition, but still falls short
While the text recognises the importance of aligning environmental goals with social protections, the absence of stronger commitments and defined mechanisms limits its transformative potential.
 
   
 
Global Plastic Profiles 2025: Article 9 points to legacy polymer waste, but lacks ambition
While the article references science-based monitoring, cleanup efforts and stakeholder participation, the language is largely voluntary
 
   
 
Global Plastic Profiles 2025: Article 8 addresses the management of polymer waste; but differences remain
The article’s varying use of ‘shall’ and ‘should’ continues to create ambiguity around enforceability.
 
   
 
Global Plastic Profiles 2025: Article 6 aims to guide states toward more sustainable production systems
But positions are far from uniform, with some states supporting expansion of its scope and others questioning its compatibility with the core agenda
 
   
 
Global Plastic Profiles 2025: Article 5 recognises importance of product design but is misaligned with ambition
While it outlines obligations for Parties to enhance product design, it lacks clarity on minimum global criteria or harmonized performance requirements
 
   
 
Global Plastic Profiles 2025: Article 3 is most ambitious, but remains heavily bracketed
At its core, the article attempts to reconcile national flexibility with the need for global coherence in addressing high-risk plastic products and chemicals of concern
 
   
 
Global Plastic Profiles 2025: Talks on Article 1 reveal broad consensus on ending polymer pollution
Most countries agree that the global plastic treaty should explicitly aim to protect human health and the environment, with many emphasising the need for a comprehensive, full life-cycle approach to plastics

Africa’s climate adaption: Mozambique moves to tackle climate change development challenges
