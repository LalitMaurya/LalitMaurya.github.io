Africa’s climate adaption: How Zimbabwe is reimagining agriculture as it goes from being bread basket to food importer
Climate change-related conditions such as heatwaves, droughts & floods are leading to reduced crop yields, sanitation problems, disrupted power generation and wildlife crisis. Now, efforts are underway towards climate adaptation
 
   
 
Healthy diets now a luxury for billions: Cost of healthy diets 47% higher than ultra-processed foods
SOFI 2025 report finds 2.6 billion people cannot afford nutritious meals as food inflation drives up prices of fruits, vegetables and animal-based foods
 
 
   
 
Ramsar COP15 opens in Zimbabwe with urgent call to reverse wetland decline
Over 3,000 delegates from 172 countries to address the alarming decline of wetlands, which have decreased by 35% since 1970
 
   
 
Ramsar COP15: Experts stress importance of wildlife to Africa’s wetland preservation
An expert report calls for inclusion of wildlife-driven ecological processes as a way of arresting the ongoing degradation that is threatening the African continent’s wetlands
 
   
 
Around 8.2% of world’s population affected by hunger last year: SOFI 2025
An estimated 96 million more people were suffering from chronic hunger today compared to 2015, according to the report
 
   
 
Heatwaves aggravate urban air pollution in sub-Saharan Africa, study finds
New research from Kigali highlights risks of compounding climate and pollution crises
 

 
   
   
 
Atlas identifies areas rich in mycorrhizal fungi, some 90% of them are outside protected areas
There is an urgent need to protect mycorrhizal fungi to improve soil quality and sequester carbon
 
   
 
Renewables most cost-effective option for new electricity generation: IRENA
Renewables cheaper than fossil fuels worldwide; On-shore wind projects remained the cheapest source of renewable electricity in 2024, followed by solar PV
 
   
 
Meet the Black Mambas, South Africa’s all-women force to prevent rhino poaching
More than a decade ago, the Black Mambas were brought together to tackle rhino poaching in South Africa. The group constituting women, mostly from the villages, created a new dimension in wildlife conservation
 
   
 
African sci-fi imagines new ways of living in climate-changed worlds
African climate fiction is especially good at showing how past, present and future overlap, and how humanity relates to nonhuman and more-than-human ways of being.
